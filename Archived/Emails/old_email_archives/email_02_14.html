A word in memory is unaligned if it crosses a word boundary.  For example,
locations 12 and 13 are inside a word bounday.  Locations 13 and 14 are not.
A simple test for word boundaries: Do address bits other than the low order
bit change between the two addresses.

	12   00001100		13   00001101
	13   00001101           14   00001110
	     -                       ^
	       No			  Yes

A word in aligned memory consists of the bytes at addresses A and A+1, where
A is an even number.  We will see why that is important when we talk about 
physical memory next week.

As to the notion of unalighed byte loads and stores, there is no such thing in
a byte-addressable memory.  Bytes can be stored or loaded from any location 
without any problem.  We will see why next week when we study physical memory.
